About

Elevator Pitches

For Scientists

I study turfgrass quality control, focusing on differentiating Poa annua and creeping bentgrass in golf course systems. Using remote sensing tools, including handheld and drone‑based hyperspectral and multispectral cameras, I aim to detect stress before it becomes visually apparent. I also look at how drought, heat, and traffic interact and how to separate their spectral signatures. By comparing ground‑based and aerial data, my goal is practical, data‑driven strategies for monitoring turf health while advancing applied research in turfgrass physiology and precision management.

For the General Public

On golf courses, two grasses compete: creeping bentgrass (smooth and fast) and Poa annua (patchy and stubborn). Problems usually show up after damage is done. I use drones and specialized cameras to spot early stress—before our eyes can see it. Catch issues early, fix them sooner, save water and money, and keep greens consistent. It’s an early‑warning system for turf.

For Industry / Government / Funders

Superintendents spend heavily controlling Poa annua and protecting creeping bentgrass under heat, drought, and traffic. I’m building remote‑sensing workflows (handheld and drone) that flag pre‑visual stress and estimate Poa density/seedheads. Earlier, targeted decisions reduce pesticide, water, and labor inputs while improving playability. Deliverable: low‑friction protocols and sensor guidance that translate to daily operations.
